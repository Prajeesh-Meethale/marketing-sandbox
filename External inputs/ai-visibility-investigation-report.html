Module 04 — The Machine Layer

What AI actually sees when it looks at you

This part costs nothing to run and takes seconds — so we run it first. It's the site's own contribution to the problem.

Machines can't find the front door

MechanismNo llms.txt or robots.txt blocking — crawlers can enter, but nothing tells them what matters most on the site.
Business costModels fall back on whatever the wider web says about Sleepy Owl instead of what the brand would say about itself.
Fix: publish llms.txt ★ Quick win · Low effort

Machines find you, but can't confidently repeat what you do

MechanismAI models cite sources that already read like an answer. Sleepy Owl's pages read like a brochure, not evidence.
Business costThis is why competitors get quoted directly in AI answers, and Sleepy Owl gets summarized vaguely — or skipped.
Fix: rewrite top 5 pages to open with a one-line direct answer, add FAQ schema Medium effort

Machines might be seeing a blank page

MechanismKey pages render via JavaScript with no server-side or noscript fallback — a bot that doesn't execute JS may see an empty shell.
Business costContent already invested in may functionally not exist to a meaningful share of the crawlers hitting the site.
Fix: SSR or noscript fallback on buyer-facing pages High effort · needs a dev
✓ Nothing else is holding Sleepy Owl back. HTTPS, page weight, and language declaration all pass — the technical foundation is sound. This was never a "broken website" problem.

Final Assessment

The opportunity isn't to become more visible. It's to become understandable.

Sleepy Owl has the product and the credibility — they're just disconnected from each other in a way AI can't parse yet. Every gap in this file is fixable without new content: mostly better packaging of what already exists. The Machine Layer findings alone cost nothing to act on and nothing to re-run. That's the fastest lever available before touching anything else.
